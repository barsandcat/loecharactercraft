Hand of Justice
Only registered members of the Executor guild are allowed to wear
this symbol, and they often go out of their way to display it on even
the smallest piece of their equipment.
To simple people of the realm, the hand of justice helps tell the licensed Executors apart from simple bounty hunters and mercenaries
who are not bound by the guild’s laws. To those with a contract on
them, this symbol is a reminder that whoever you are, and wherever
you hide, the hand of justice will find a way to reach you.

Moonshield
Paladin
A young member of the Moonshield order, you swore to protect the
people and the law of Evershade just like Luada does, at any cost.
With the strict knights’ code binding your hands, you’re not well suited
to treacherous or dishonorable acts. But the same code makes people
more likely to trust you and causes simple folks to see you as their savior.
Keywords:
Leadership – People tend to naturally follow your lead.
Advancement Trees: As a Moonshield, you will be able to pick Advancements from your own tree as well as the Warfare and Battle Magic trees.

The Book of the Code
For the Paladins of the Moonshield Order, the Code is
the highest authority on all matters. A book containing it, apart from their titular Moonshield, is something a Paladin must always carry. It is said the Code
was passed from Luada herself and represents her perfect, intended way for the people of Evershade to live.
However, many people of Evershade beg to differ.

Omu Priest
As a priest of Omu’s order, you do not merely believe your god still lives.
You know it, as you find his signs all across Evershade. You have vowed
to continue the centuries-long search for the lost deity, and to preserve
his domain along the way.
Your wisdom, and the remnants of your god’s miraculous power, let you
aid your allies through all manner of trials: physical, moral, and strategic. However, you are not best suited to direct confrontation. After all,
there are wiser ways to resolve conflicts.
Keywords:
Lore – Preserving knowledge is your sacred duty. Using it to aid your
cause is a boon that follows.
Advancement Trees: As an Omu Priest, you will be able to pick Advancements from your own tree as well as the Support Magic and
Scholarship trees.

Flame of Wisdom
Upon taking their vows, a newly ordained Omu priest receives a special gift – a lantern-like vial of ever-burning flame, a symbol of undying
wisdom. Its flame rises when the cleric acts wisely and wanes whenever they yield to ignorance or folly. It is said that a flame strong enough
may guide the way to Omu, yet one should beware of gazing into the
fire too long, lest it enthrall them.

Scoundrel
In the overcrowded cities and forgotten regions of Evershade, theft,
deception, and fraud are sometimes the only ways to survive. In that
regard, the back alleys of a slum differ little from the polished halls of
noble courts.
Nimble fingers, fast reflexes, and quick thinking serve you equally well
both in and out of combat. That is your way of dealing with the world –
getting what you want and vanishing as if you were never there.
Keywords:
Deception – You can pretend to be just about anyone, and you find it
easy to blend into any kind of group.
Advancement Trees: As a Scoundrel, you will be able to pick Advancements from your own tree as well as the Subterfuge and Thiefcraft trees.

Smuggler’s Sleeve
A true Scoundrel is not afraid to make bets with uncertain outcomes.
And when things don’t go their way, sometimes the moment calls for
a bad solution: a cleverly hidden, tiny blade. It is hard to spot and quick
to use – perfect to get rid of snoops or inconvenient witnesses. The
sleeve itself also proves useful for concealing what should not be found.
